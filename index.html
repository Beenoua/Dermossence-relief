Hemorrhoids are classified based on their location and severity. Understanding the type you have is the first step toward finding the right treatment.

  • Internal: Form inside the rectum. Usually painless but can bleed. They are graded by severity:
    • Grade I: Bleeds but does not prolapse (bulge out).
    • Grade II: Prolapses but retracts on its own.
    • Grade III: Prolapses and must be manually pushed back in.
    • Grade IV: Permanently prolapsed and cannot be pushed back.
  • External: Form under the skin around the anus. Can cause itching, irritation, and pain.
  • Thrombosed: Occurs when a blood clot forms in an external hemorrhoid, causing sudden, severe pain and a hard lump.

Symptomatic hemorrhoids are caused by factors that increase pressure on the veins in the lower rectum. The most common contributors include:

  • Straining during bowel movements due to constipation.
  • Prolonged sitting on the toilet, which allows blood to pool.
  • Low-fiber diet and poor hydration leading to hard stools.
  • Obesity, which increases abdominal pressure.
  • Pregnancy, due to fetal pressure and hormonal changes.
  • Heavy lifting and a sedentary lifestyle.

While symptoms vary, several hallmark signs indicate the presence of hemorrhoids. It is crucial to get a proper diagnosis as these can overlap with other conditions.

  • Painless Bleeding: Bright red blood on toilet paper or in the toilet bowl is the most common sign of internal hemorrhoids.
  • Itching & Irritation: A very common and distressing symptom, especially with external hemorrhoids.
  • Pain & Discomfort: Typically associated with external or thrombosed hemorrhoids, often worse when sitting.
  • Swelling or Lump: A palpable lump around the anus is a key sign of an external or prolapsed hemorrhoid.
